Lesson 1.1: Hello, I Am Ben (Introducing the Verb 'To Be')

This introductory lesson focuses on the communication goals of greeting others and providing basic personal information, such as name and place of origin. Mastering the verb **'To Be'** is paramount, as it serves as the most fundamental building block for English sentence construction.

Dialogue: Meet Ben and Anna

Anna: Hello! My name is Anna. What is your name?

Ben: Hi, Anna! My name is Ben. Nice to meet you!

Anna: Nice to meet you too, Ben. Where are you from?

Ben: I am from Canada. I am a student.

Anna: Oh, that is cool! I am a teacher. I am from Iran.

Grammar Explanation: The Verb 'To Be' (Am, Is, Are)

The verb 'To Be' is used at the A1 level to define, describe, and state location. For example, a learner uses this verb to state their job ("I am a teacher") or their origin ("I am from Canada").

The Three Forms and Subjects:

The verb 'To Be' changes form depending on the subject (I, He, She, It, We, You, They):

  • **I** uses **am** (e.g., I am Ben.)
  • **He/She/It** uses **is** (e.g., She is tall. It is cool.)
  • **You / We / They** use **are** (e.g., You are a student. We are friends.).

Contractions:

In casual conversation, the verb 'to be' is often shortened or **contracted** to sound more natural.

  • I am becomes **I'm**
  • She is becomes **She's**
  • They are becomes **They're**

Making Sentences Negative:

To form a negative sentence, the simple word **'not'** is added directly after the verb 'to be' (e.g., I am not tired. He is not from Italy.).

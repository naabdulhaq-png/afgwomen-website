Grammar Explanation: The Verb 'To Be' (Am, Is, Are)

[cite_start]

The verb 'To Be' is used at the A1 level to define, describe, and state location[cite: 47]. [cite_start]For example, a learner uses this verb to state their job ("I am a teacher") or their origin ("I am from Canada")[cite: 48, 16].

The Three Forms and Subjects:

[cite_start]

The verb 'To Be' changes form depending on the subject (I, He, She, It, We, You, They)[cite: 51]:

    [cite_start]
  • **I** uses **am** (e.g., I am Ben.) [cite: 51]
    • [cite_start]
  • **He/She/It** uses **is** (e.g., She is tall. It is cool.) [cite: 52]
    • [cite_start]
  • **You / We / They** use **are** (e.g., You are a student. We are friends.)[cite: 53, 20].

Contractions:

[cite_start]

In casual conversation, the verb 'to be' is often shortened or **contracted** to sound more natural[cite: 55, 56, 20].

    [cite_start]
  • I am becomes **I'm** [cite: 58]
    • [cite_start]
  • She is becomes **She's** [cite: 59]
    • [cite_start]
  • They are becomes **They're** [cite: 60]

Making Sentences Negative:

[cite_start]

To form a negative sentence, the simple word **'not'** is added directly after the verb 'to be' (e.g., I am not tired. He is not from Italy.)[cite: 62, 20].

Lesson 1.2: My Big Family (Basic Adjectives and Possessives)

[cite_start]

Building on Lesson 1.1, this segment expands descriptive capacity by introducing **simple adjectives** and **possessive adjectives**[cite: 89]. [cite_start]This progression allows the learner to move from merely stating identity to describing relationships and characteristics[cite: 90].

Dialogue: Anna's Family Photo

[cite_start]

The following dialogue utilizes simple subject pronouns and introduces possessive adjectives ('my', 'his') and high-frequency descriptive adjectives ('tall', 'nice', 'big', 'small')[cite: 93, 5].

[cite_start]

Ben: Who is this in the photo, Anna? [cite: 95]

Anna: That is my mother. [cite_start]She is a doctor. [cite: 96]

[cite_start]

Ben: Is she tall? [cite: 97]

Anna: Yes, she is tall and very nice. This is my father. [cite_start]He is big. [cite: 98]

[cite_start]

Ben: Do you have a brother or sister? [cite: 99]

Anna: I have one brother. His name is Ali. He is small. My brother and I are students. [cite_start]We are a happy family! [cite: 100, 101]

Grammar Explanation: Possessive Adjectives and Basic Adjectives

[cite_start]

Possessive adjectives are necessary for A1 learners to discuss belongings and relationships clearly[cite: 107, 22].

Possessive Adjectives (Showing Ownership):

[cite_start]

These words must come **before the noun** (person, place, or thing) to show who it belongs to[cite: 110, 5]. [cite_start]They are essential for answering questions like "Whose is this?"[cite: 110, 22].

    [cite_start]
  • I uses **My** (My mother) [cite: 111]
    • [cite_start]
  • You uses **Your** (Your name) [cite: 111]
    • [cite_start]
  • He uses **His** (His name) [cite: 112]
    • [cite_start]
  • She uses **Her** (Her name) [cite: 113]

Adjective Order (Description):

[cite_start]

**Adjectives** are words that describe people and things (e.g., old, big, small)[cite: 115, 5]. [cite_start]In English, the adjective nearly always comes **before the noun** it describes[cite: 115].

    [cite_start]
  • **Correct:** a happy family [cite: 116]
    • [cite_start]
  • **Incorrect:** a family happy [cite: 117]
